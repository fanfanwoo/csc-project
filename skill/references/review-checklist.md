# CSC Review Checklist

Use this checklist before treating an output as decision-ready.

## Evidence quality

- Does every top signal have a source URL and publish date?
- Is the source credible enough for the claim being made?
- Is the signal supported by the source, or is it mostly inference?
- Are old articles excluded unless they are intentionally part of historical context?

## Classification quality

- Is the domain correct?
- Is the signal type correct?
- Are relevance, impact, urgency, and novelty scores reasonable?
- Has the model avoided forcing weak items into strong categories?

## Strategic usefulness

- Does the implication tell a product/design/risk team what may change?
- Is the recommendation too generic?
- Is there a clear watch item for the next cycle?

## Risk gates

Flag for human review if the brief includes:
- Regulatory or legal interpretation.
- Lending, credit, pricing, risk appetite, privacy, or customer harm implications.
- Single-source high-impact claims.
- Contradictory or rapidly changing information.
- Any claim that could influence external communication or roadmap direction.

## Final answer standard

A good CSC output is not just a summary of news. It should explain:

```text
What changed? -> Why does it matter? -> What should we watch or decide next?
```
